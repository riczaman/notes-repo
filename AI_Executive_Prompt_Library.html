What is this?

This is a ready-to-deploy AI prompt library for enterprise HR and executive teams. It provides production-ready prompt templates that leaders can use with Microsoft Copilot, Claude, or ChatGPT to generate high-quality, strategically aligned annual and quarterly objectives — without manual HR support for every request.

Quick start guide
Three steps. Under 15 minutes. No technical knowledge required.
🔍
Step 1 — Find
Use the sidebar to navigate to your role or department. Pick the prompt that matches your use case.
✍️
Step 2 — Fill
Click Expand on the prompt. Replace every [FIELD IN BRACKETS] with your own information. Click Copy prompt.
✨
Step 3 — Generate
Paste into Microsoft Copilot (or any AI tool). Read the output. Edit and refine. Paste final text into Workday.
Where to open the prompt
💻 Microsoft Copilot (recommended)
1. Open Microsoft 365 → Copilot
2. Or go to copilot.microsoft.com
3. Paste your completed prompt
4. Press Enter and wait ~10 seconds
5. Review and copy the output
💡 Other AI tools (same steps)
Claude: claude.ai
ChatGPT: chat.openai.com
Google Gemini: gemini.google.com
All prompts work identically in any tool
Tips for the best results
🟢 Be specific — the more detail you provide in the [FIELDS], the better the AI output
🟢 Use real numbers — actual KPIs and metrics produce much stronger objectives than generic ones
🟢 Paste strategy language verbatim — copy exact wording from your corporate strategy document
🔴 AI output is a first draft — always review, edit, and make it yours before submitting
🔴 Never paste confidential salary data — keep sensitive HR data out of external AI tools
🟢 Regenerate if needed — type "Try again with more ambitious targets" or "Make objective 3 more specific"
🟢 Ask for alternatives — "Give me 3 alternative versions of objective 2" works well

Risks and mitigation
Security, compliance, and delivery risk register
Security and compliance
Data sensitivity
Prompts contain no PII. Users must not paste individual performance data or salaries into external AI tools. Acceptable use policy required.
Copilot data residency
Microsoft Copilot for M365 uses tenant-scoped data with enterprise data protection. No training on customer data. Confirm tenant config with IT.
External AI tools
For Claude/ChatGPT fallback: use enterprise accounts where available. Update acceptable use policy accordingly.
SharePoint permissions
Prompt library is internal only. No anonymous access. All access via corporate identity (Entra ID).
AI output accuracy
All AI output is clearly a draft requiring human review. No AI output goes to Workday without leader approval in MVP phase.
Risk register
High
Low adoption — leaders revert to HR
Mitigation: Executive sponsor mandate. HR actively directs all requests to the tool. CPO tracks adoption monthly.
High
AI output quality below expectation
Mitigation: Pilot prompts with 10 executives first. Iterate before broad rollout. Governance committee reviews quality monthly.
Medium
Users paste sensitive data into external AI
Mitigation: Acceptable use policy. Training. Prompts use placeholders that guide safe inputs.
Medium
Prompt library becomes stale
Mitigation: Annual mandatory review. Strategy pivots trigger ad hoc review. Version status visible on every prompt.
Medium
Copilot license gaps
Mitigation: Free fallback options documented. Power Automate flow for non-licensed users in Phase 3.
Low
SharePoint governance overhead
Mitigation: Lightweight governance in MVP. Power Automate handles approvals automatically. Monthly not weekly cadence.
Low
Workday AI launches sooner than expected
Mitigation: Prompt library is an asset not a liability. Prompts, governance model and feedback data all transfer to Workday AI onboarding.
